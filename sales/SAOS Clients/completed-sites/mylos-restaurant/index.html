The Venetian Water Mill

1960

A 14th Century Mill
Becomes a Legend

In the fertile lands of Platanias, just 11 kilometers west of Chania, stands a 14th century Venetian water mill that has witnessed centuries of Cretan life. In 1960, Dimitris Kavros saw beauty where others saw only ruins. He transformed the old mill and its surrounding inn into what would become one of the most beloved restaurants on the island.

Named "O Mylos tou Kerata" — The Mill of the Cuckold — after local folklore, the restaurant has been a gathering place for families, lovers, and travelers for over six decades. Every stone, every vine-covered wall tells a story. Here, Cretan hospitality is not a concept — it is a way of being.

Tastes of Crete

Our menu is a celebration of Cretan culinary tradition — recipes passed down through generations, prepared with the finest local ingredients and served with the warmth of genuine hospitality.

Cretan Meatpie

Mpoureki

The Experience

Where Centuries Whisper

  • The Venetian Mill

    Dine within the walls of a 14th century water mill, where the ancient mechanism still stands as a monument to Cretan craftsmanship.

  • Vine-Covered Terraces

    Under canopies of ancient grapevines, stone-walled gardens unfold into intimate dining spaces bathed in warm evening light.

  • Candlelit Evenings

    As twilight settles over Platanias, candlelight dances across stone arches and the warm Cretan breeze carries the scent of rosemary and thyme.

  • Living History

    Every corner reveals artifacts from centuries past — olive presses, millstones, and tools of a bygone era woven into the fabric of the dining experience.
