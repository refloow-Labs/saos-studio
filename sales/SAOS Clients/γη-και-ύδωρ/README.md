## What Was Built

A fully self-contained, single-file landing page (`index.html`) designed to serve as a demo pitch for the client. No frameworks, no build step — pure HTML, CSS, and vanilla JS with Google Fonts via CDN.

### Sections

1. **Hero** — Full-viewport with layered CSS gradient (earth-to-water depth), animated water ripple rings, scroll-reveal animated headline
2. **About** — Two-column grid with overlapping images, rating badge, brand pillars
3. **Menu Highlights** — Responsive masonry-style grid of 5 dishes with hover reveal descriptions; Unsplash food photography
4. **Atmosphere / Gallery** — Dark earth-toned section with two auto-scrolling horizontal image tracks (CSS animation, pause on hover)
5. **Philosophy** — Split layout: full-bleed image left / "earth & water duality" cards right
6. **Contact & Hours** — Address, phone, hours table, rating badge, clickable map placeholder linking to Google Maps
7. **Footer** — Dark ink background, navigation columns, SAOS Studio credit

### Interactions

- Intersection Observer scroll-reveal on all major elements (4 animation styles: fade-up, fade-left, fade-right, scale-in)
- Staggered delay classes (.d1–.d6) for sequenced reveals within sections
- Navbar becomes frosted glass on scroll
- Active nav link highlights as user scrolls through sections
- Hero background subtle parallax on scroll
- Gallery tracks pause animation on hover
- Menu item hover reveals description text
- Mobile hamburger menu

## Brand Direction

### Core Concept: Earth & Water Duality

The restaurant name "γη & ύδωρ" is Ancient Greek for "earth and water" — the two foundational elements of Greek cooking philosophy. Every design decision stems from this duality:

- **Earth** = terracotta (#C17A5A), warm brown (#8B5E3C), sage green (#7A8C6E), cream (#F5EFE3)
- **Water** = muted Aegean blue (#5B7FA6), deep navy (#2C5A82)
- The hero gradient literally moves from deep earth (dark brown) up through terracotta to a water-infused sky tone

### Typography

- **Cormorant Garamond** (serif) — editorial, classical, evocative of Greek tradition. Used for all headlines, dish names, large display text. The italic weight carries emotional weight.
- **Inter** (sans) — clean, modern, legible. Used for body text, labels, navigation. The contrast between the two creates personality without trying too hard.

### Visual Language

- Organic, natural — no hard corporate lines
- CSS grain texture overlay on entire page (opacity 0.028) — adds warmth and tactility
- Animated water ripple rings in hero (pure CSS, no JS)
- Earth dot-grid pattern overlaid on hero
- Wave SVG dividers between sections to avoid hard section breaks
- Horizontal auto-scroll gallery — evokes flipping through a restaurant's Instagram without the friction
- Dark earth-toned atmosphere section creates a dramatic mid-page contrast

### Photography Strategy

All Unsplash stock via direct URL. Selections favor:
- Warm tones, natural light
- Greek/Mediterranean food aesthetic
- Interior restaurant atmosphere
