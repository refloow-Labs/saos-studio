1. Scope of Work

The Designer agrees to provide web design and development services as described in the attached Scope of Work and Proposal. Deliverables typically include custom-designed webpage(s), responsive HTML/CSS/JS code, basic SEO configuration, and deployment assistance.

2. Timeline

  • Draft delivery: Within 48 hours of receiving deposit and all required content.
  • Revision period: 3 business days per revision round.
  • Final delivery: Within the timeframe specified in the proposal (typically 2–7 days).
  • Delays caused by Client (late content, unresponsive feedback) extend the timeline proportionally.

4. Revisions

The package includes the number of revision rounds specified in the proposal. Additional revisions beyond this scope are billed at €75/hour or quoted separately. "Revision" means changes to existing design elements, not entirely new concepts or pages.

5. Client Responsibilities

  • Provide all content (text, images, logo, brand guidelines) before work begins.
  • Provide timely feedback (within 3 business days of draft delivery).
  • Secure domain and hosting if not purchased through the Designer.
  • Obtain all necessary rights to provided materials (images, trademarks, copy).

6. Intellectual Property

Upon final payment, the Client receives full ownership of the final website code and design for their business use. The Designer retains the right to showcase the work in portfolio and marketing materials unless a mutual NDA prohibits it. The Designer retains rights to any proprietary tools, frameworks, or processes used.

7. Termination

Either party may terminate with 7 days written notice. If terminated by Client after draft delivery, the deposit is retained. If terminated by Designer for cause (non-payment, abusive conduct), all payments are retained and work stops immediately.

8. Limitation of Liability

The Designer's total liability shall not exceed the total project fee. The Designer is not liable for loss of profits, data loss, or business interruption. The Designer does not guarantee specific business results (leads, sales, rankings) from the website.

9. Confidentiality

Both parties agree to keep confidential any proprietary business information shared during the project, except as required for project execution or by law.
