A

The Wordmark, sculpted

Your serif "saos" extruded into a 3D glass/marble sculpture. Liquid material with subtle refraction and chromatic aberration. The word IS the art. Each scroll section, the letters morph state — crystalline, liquid, dissolving into particles, reforming. The script "studio." floats below as a delicate signature.

Why — Most on-brand. Most distinctive. Your name becomes the art piece.
Tech: extrude SVG path → MeshTransmissionMaterial in R3F.
B

Glass Monolith

A vertical refractive slab — like a polished standing stone made of crystal. Light bends through it; the shader universe behind warps and shifts. As you scroll, the slab rotates, fractures into pieces and reassembles, then dissolves. Kubrick / Brancusi.

Why — Most cinematic. Pure abstraction. No copy. Lets the brand voice breathe.
C

Liquid Orb

An organic liquid sphere — a single living metaball. Always breathing, never static. As you scroll it deforms (stretches, splits into satellites, reforms), changes material (mercury → glass → ink → smoke), and pulls light through itself. Most "alive."

Why — Most organic. Most "alive." Closer to motion-design / fluid simulation territory.

My read — Option A is the answer for "websites that look like art" because the work IS the brand. Option B has the strongest pure cinema. Option C is the most playful/alive. We can also do a hybrid: A in the hero, then it transforms into an orb (C) mid-scroll, then back to letters at the end.
