Three creative directions for the 3D rebuild

Each preserves your editorial dark aesthetic and the soft blue accent — but interprets "websites that look like art" through a different 3D lens. Click the one that feels most "you."

A

Cinematic Flythrough

A camera glides through a dark architectural 3D space as you scroll. Each section becomes a "chapter" the camera dollies into — type emerges from the void, dissolves, the camera moves on. Volumetric light, fine particles, restrained.

References — Apple AirPods Pro · Igloo Inc · Bruno Simon's editorial work
B

Floating Editorial Gallery

Sections exist as glass-like panels suspended in 3D space — a museum in zero gravity. The camera orbits and dollies between them as you scroll. Each "panel" is a piece in a curated exhibition. Most aligned with "websites as art."

References — Linear gallery views · Lusion · Cabana
SAOS.
C

Liquid Shader Universe

A single procedural GLSL shader fills the background — a slow, liquid universe that morphs and shifts color as you scroll. Glass-treated type and content float above on a flat plane. Most abstract, most "art-piece," lightest performance footprint.

References — Linear hero · Vercel marketing · Awwwards SOTD

Note — All three would use React Three Fiber + drei + GSAP ScrollTrigger, with full mobile fallback to a refined 2D version (so we don't ship a slow site on phones). I can also propose a hybrid if none feels right alone.
