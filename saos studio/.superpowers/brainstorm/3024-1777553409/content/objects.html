Does the shader universe contain anything?

You picked the Liquid Shader Universe. Now: pure abstract, one centerpiece, or a populated world. This is the "go extra" lever.

A

Pure Atmosphere

No 3D objects. Just the shader universe behind glass-treated content cards. The art is the atmosphere itself — slow color drifts, light scattering, maybe a fine particle haze. Most restrained. Closest to "elegant minimalism."

Vibe — editorial · contemplative · safe
a single, living object
B

Living Centerpiece

One 3D sculpture lives in the universe — a morphing object built from your SAOS logo (or a refined liquid monolith). It rotates, distorts, and changes state as you scroll. Sections are "moments around the object." It IS your brand made dimensional.

Vibe — sculptural · iconic · gallery-worthy
Most on-brand for "websites that look like art"
C

Populated Universe

Multiple sculptural objects float in the universe — each section reveals a different "artifact" that represents that section's idea. Approach has a rotating ring, Services has a cluster of monoliths, etc. Most "extra." More to design, more to optimize.

Vibe — maximalist · curated museum · ambitious

My read — Option B is the strongest answer to "websites that look like art." A single iconic object becomes a meme — people screenshot it, share it. Option C risks looking like a generic "we put 3D in everything" portfolio. But if you want to go fully maximalist, C is yours.
